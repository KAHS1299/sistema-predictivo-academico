Interpretation

The K-Means algorithm segmented the dataset into three clusters based on product sales and profit. Each cluster represents a distinct behavioral pattern. Centroids indicate the average position of each segment, supporting decision-making in pricing and sales strategies.

Context of the Data

This dataset consists of 1,000 simulated retail records. It tracks the relationship between the number of units sold and the net profit generated by different store locations.

Interpretation of Results

  • Cluster 0 (Low Volume): Smaller locations with sales below 50 units. Ideal for growth-focused strategies.
  • Cluster 1 (Standard Operations): Mid-range stores with consistent profit margins.
  • Cluster 2 (Top Performers): High-volume locations that drive the bulk of the company's revenue.
